Hands-On Workshop on Computer Vision Using OpenCV

Date: August 20, 2024 | Time: 11:00 AM - 4:10 PM | Venue: BSAU Apple iOS Application Development Center

The workshop, organized by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, introduced 35 students to key concepts of computer vision. Facilitators M.B. Ajwadh Jalal and Aadel Mohamed Rabeek guided participants through image and video processing techniques using Python OpenCV, culminating in a hands-on project on contour and shape detection.

Participants engaged in practical sessions covering topics like contour and shape detection, and a live demonstration of image classification using YOLOv8. By the end of the workshop, attendees had a hands-on understanding of how to use OpenCV for real-world image processing tasks.

Event Agenda

  • 11:00 AM - 11:30 AM: Introduction by M.B. Ajwadh Jalal
  • 11:30 AM - 12:30 PM: Hands-on Image Processing using OpenCV by Aadel Mohamed Rabeek
  • 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM: Lunch Break
  • 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM: Introduction to YOLOv8 Image Classification
  • 3:00 PM - 4:00 PM: Hands-on Project: Contour and Shape Detection
  • 4:00 PM - 4:10 PM: Q&A and Wrap-up

Workshop Outcome

  • Participants understood basic computer vision concepts.
  • Practical demonstration of image classification using YOLOv8.
  • Hands-on project on contour and shape detection.

Attendee Feedback

Participants were highly satisfied with the workshop, particularly appreciating the hands-on activities and practical examples. Several attendees expressed their interest in future events related to computer vision and OpenCV.
