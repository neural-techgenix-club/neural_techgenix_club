Workshop on Introduction to Computer Vision

Date: March 28, 2024 | Time: 10:00 AM - 12:30 PM | Venue: Web Technology Lab, CSE

This workshop, organized by the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, introduced the core concepts of computer vision. Conducted on March 28, 2024, the workshop was attended by 52 students from the B.Tech. CSE, AI&DS, and Cyber Security programs. Dr. James Immanuel, Lead Data Scientist at EinNel Technologies, led the session, which provided hands-on techniques in image processing using OpenCV.

The workshop began with a brief introduction by Mr. Albert Einstein, CEO of EinNel Technologies, emphasizing the importance of real-time skills in computer vision. Dr. James Immanuel then led the session, covering topics such as image representation, human vs. computer vision, pattern recognition, and the use of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in image processing. A real-time image processing system was demonstrated, showcasing the practical application of these models.

Event Agenda

  • 10:00 AM - 10:30 AM: Inauguration by Mr. Albert Einstein
  • 10:30 AM - 11:30 AM: Introduction to Computer Vision by Dr. James Immanuel
  • 11:30 AM - 12:00 PM: Hands-on Image Processing with OpenCV
  • 12:00 PM - 12:30 PM: Q&A and Real-Time System Demonstration

Workshop Outcome

  • Participants gained knowledge of core computer vision concepts and image processing techniques.
  • Attendees learned about the stages of image processing, human vision comparison, and CNN models.
  • The workshop provided practical insights into real-time applications of computer vision systems.

Attendee Feedback

Participants expressed satisfaction with the workshop, appreciating the practical examples and hands-on sessions. Many expressed interest in future workshops on similar topics.
